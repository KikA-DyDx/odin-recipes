Egusi Soup

Description

Egusi soup is unarguably the most popular Nigerian soup.
The Yorubas make efo elegusi while the Igbos make ofe egusi, all with similar ingredients but with slightly different approaches.

Ingredients

  • Salt to taste
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1KG or 2.2lb beef
  • 4 cups of egusi (melon)
  • 1lb or 500g Roasted fish.
  • Half a cup of ground crayfish
  • Pepper to taste (scotch bonnet).
  • 20g Dawadawa or opkei (local ingredients)
  • One medium-sized Stock fish head (okporoko)
  • A handful of sliced ugu (fluted pumpkin) leaves

Steps

  1. Grind the Egusi: Grind four cups of egusi into a fine powder, mix with a cup of water to create a thick paste, and set aside.
  2. Parboil the Meat: Season and parboil the meat for ten minutes with seasoning cubes, salt, and onions. Add water and cook until tender, leaving a small amount of stock.
  3. Prepare the Fish: Soak stockfish in hot water, wash thoroughly, and set aside. Clean and debone the roasted fish.
  4. Cook the Egusi Paste: Heat palm oil in a pot, add the egusi paste, and stir for 8-10 minutes until it forms seed-like crumbs.
  5. Combine Ingredients: Add the cooked meat, dry fish, stockfish, crayfish, seasoning cube, scotch bonnet pepper, and water. Stir well and let boil for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Add dawadawa, salt, and pepper to taste. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  7. Add Utazi Leaves: Add sliced utazi leaves, simmer on low heat for 2 minutes, stir, and the egusi soup is ready.
